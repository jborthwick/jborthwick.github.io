Ten Percent Happier

a case study

I had the privilege of leading product design at Ten Percent Happier between 2017 and 2023, collaborating closely with product, engineering and content teams to ship impact-driven, human-centered designs.

In 2022, Ten Percent Happier wanted to help subscribers find the content they love more quickly, instead of getting lost in its vast library. Furthermore, the existing app structure had stagnated, lacking easy ways to experiment with bringing their unique brand of mindfulness to life.

üèÄ High Level Goals

  • Create a foundational Home experience to test, learn and iterate on.
  • Encourage depth of engagement through a broader array of content.
  • Better guide brand new members into starting a meditation practice with Ten Percent Happier.
  • Showcase our difference with stories that highlights our impact, teachers, and community.

üìä Key Questions & KPIs

  • Does the Home experience improve trial conversion rates?
  • Does the Home experience positively impact retention?
  • Does Home improve app engagement rates?
  • What content on Home is most appealing?

üè° What We Built

We landed on a dual-track experience: the first geared toward new users during their trial period, and a second toward existing subscribers.

New meditators would be guided through a 4-week experience highlighting the best performing content. Progression through The Basics would be front and center, as it continually outperformed other courses during a user's trial period. In qualitative interviews, user's often attributed their first "Ah-hah" moment to The Basics, and it highly correlated to activation and trial conversion. Next, users could explore a limited batch of beginner-friendly meditations. While new users were often overwhelmed with the app's large library of meditations, smoke tests had shown surfacing small relevant packs positively impacted M1 > M2 retention. This one-two punch of content would be paired with a series of weekly articles, each packed with insights and content recommendations, modeled after their successful newsletter. And finally, if a user wanted to dig deeper, a selection from their award-winning podcasts could be found.

Existing users would have a broader, more personalized experience. If a user was in the middle of a course, they could instantly launch their next session. A popular meditation of the day feature would be revamped, better contextualized and made available for users looking for a quick fix. Weekly articles, modeled after their succcesful newsletter, would explore an array of topics, bringing fresh insights and content recommendations. New dynamic meditation packs would highlight timely, high-performing meditations. Easy Access to a user's Favorites and Recently Played, both highly used yet buried features, would also be included. Finally, a latent referall program would be given a much more prominent home.

Most importantly, a new foundation to iterate from would be built, a key strategic initiative as Ten Percent Happier steered out of the choppy post-pandemic waters.

üå± How We Did

Initial multivariate tests were launched in the fall of 2022 to observe impact and mitigate risk. In mid-December, the new Home experience fully rolled out, just in time for Ten Percent Happier's biggest launch of the year: The Dalai Lama's Guide to Happiness.

  • Trial conversion rate for new users increased by 5%.
  • Day 3 Activation of The Basics increased by 12%.
  • Referall use increased by 13%.
  • 15% engagement increase with additiaonal content types.
  • Subscriber renewal rates maintained.
