Pixels & Pancakes

portfolio of ui, ux and other fine acronyms

by JEREMY BORTHWICK

I’m a designer by trade and a builder at heart. I love to figure out what makes something tick, so I can make it work better. I grew up in the swamps of Florida before escaping to the blizzards of Boston, where I lead projects for national brands like Fidelity, MINI, Harvard, and Saucony, along with local nonprofits like the Greater Boston Food Bank and Franciscan Children’s Hospital. And on Saturday’s I make delicious pancakes for my son and daughter.

Now I lead product design at Mapkin, where we’re building the first friendly GPS. Along with crafting all UI & UX design, I help write the rules engine that drives the voice navigation.

Mapkin

What if a GPS guided you like a friend? Riding shotgun, they'd say "Take a right at the light, just past the library", not "Turn right right in 750 feet". That's Mapkin.

With an incredibly small team, we built a nation-wide GPS app with intuitive turn-by-turn directions based on analyzing OSM map data, on-the-ground seeding efforts and tips from Mapkin users. We incorporated visual landmarks, local tips, lane guidance, warnings about tricky intersections, friendly reminders, and more.

On top of that, we gave it an incredibly simple interface, designed to get you going fast while safe and easy to use on the road.

At launch, Mapkin was immediately featured by Apple. Within 6 months, drivers had passed over 2 million miles.

FRANCISCAN

Franciscan deserved a site that showcased their dedication, passion and unrivaled breadth in serving the physical, mental and educational needs of a diverse community of kids. Weaving rich photography with real, compelling success stories, and a more inviting brand refresh, I led UX and design for a new franciscanhospital.org.
